Overview

This repository contains a step-by-step proof of the Inverse Function Theorem, including both the special case and the general case, developed from self-study notes for learning, review, and deeper conceptual understanding.

The purpose of these notes is not merely to present a proof, but to show how difficult mathematical ideas can be constructed from simple principles through disciplined reasoning. Beginning with continuity, linear approximations, and contraction mappings, the argument builds gradually toward one of the foundational results of advanced calculus.

A central theme of this project is that sophisticated mathematics often becomes accessible when broken into small, justified steps.

	•	Reduce difficult problems into manageable pieces
	•	Use structure rather than guesswork

That same reasoning also informs aspects of my investment thinking, particularly in how small asymmetries, incremental adjustments, and disciplined decision-making can compound into larger outcomes over time.

Why This Proof Matters

The proof shows how a major theorem can emerge from relatively elementary tools, including:
	•	Continuity
	•	The Mean Value Theorem
	•	Matrix invertibility
	•	Differentiability
	•	Banach’s Fixed Point Theorem

Rather than treating the theorem as a black box, these notes aim to make the logic transparent.
